Photography

I'm not a professional photographer and I definitely don't own a DSLR but whipping out my phone to take photos has become a norm for me, especially with the rise of social media. As I scroll through my phone gallery, there are some rather common themes among the photos present and I would like to share the top three things that I enjoy photographing.


1. Gifts


I realised that I really really like to take photos of gifts from my friends but to spare you the trouble of scrolling through the tons of photos, I shan't post all of them here. (JK I'm just lazy to upload them) The above image were gifts from my friends who went overseas: the Dalecarlian horse from Sweden, a pair of cat plushies with one of them wearing a red scrunchie and the packs of milk tea from Malaysia, the cardboard box containing a delicate metal bookmark from China, the cow plushie from Japan and the nougat green tea biscuit from Taiwan.


2. Food

Surprise surprise. As one of my motivations to continue living, food definitely plays a huge role in reducing my phone's available memory. I like to photograph all kinds of food, be it the trashy dining hall food that makes people wonder where in the world did the money they paid for their meal plan disappear to (here's some photographic evidence of the atrocity - disclaimer it's not taken by me) or aesthetic food that I paid money for the sole purpose of taking photos and not wanting to eat it because it's too pretty.


3. Embarrassing Moments

Yeap I love videoing or photographing embarrassing moments of my friends (not myself) so that I can laugh at them when I feel sad. @Friends don't worry your secret is safe with me. I won't post them here but I'll keep them to myself as treasured memories to hold on to (and laugh about).
